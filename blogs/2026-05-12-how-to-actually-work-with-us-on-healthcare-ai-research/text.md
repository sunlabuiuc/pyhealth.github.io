**How to Actually Work With Us on Healthcare AI Research**

Jimeng and I get some version of this email almost every week:

> Dear John/Professor Sun, I want to work on meaningful healthcare AI research. I have a background in \[X\]. Please guide me.

We appreciate the enthusiasm, genuinely. But we already have a structured path for exactly this: the **PyHealth Research Initiative**.

Here's how it works, and what we actually look for.

***

**The two things you need to do**

**1. Join the Discord.** Everything lives there — announcements, bounty lists, the `#review-requests` channel. Find the link at[ pyhealth.dev](https://pyhealth.dev).

**2. Submit a quality PR to PyHealth.** Browse the bounty lists, pick something that interests you, and ping us in `#review-requests` when you're ready.

- 📋[ PyHealth Paper Bounties](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/10PdymbwbsgqPMjBcJIukrDjUNZNlCwuUGbRyr2xCIBo/edit?usp=sharing)

- ⚡[ PyHealth 2.0 Bounty List (Extra Credit & Industry Connections)](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1ruzKu-sTUnGZ3i9JPXzvjVCvxRFn6QJv2YLtVdXsmuE/edit?usp=sharing)

***

**Why a PR, and not a CV?**

**We can't teach you from scratch.** Research time is scarce. A solid PR tells us you can write clean code, actually read a paper, and break it down technically. That's exactly what research is: roughly 95% reproduction and pipelining, 5% exploration. We need to know you can do the 95%.

**Your CV doesn't tell us what we need to know.** A list of courses and internships can't show us whether you're self-driven, whether you'll see a project through to the end, or whether you actually care about this work, but a PR does. It shows us you're willing to invest your time in our project before we invest ours in yours.

***

**TL;DR**

Join the Discord, submit a PR. That combo is what keeps you on our radar — not an email that gets buried in a very, very long Illinois inbox.
